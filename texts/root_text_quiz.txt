CHAPTER 1: IN PRAISE OF BODHICITTA

Q1. To whom does Śāntideva bow down at the very beginning of the text?

b) The Lion of the Śākyas
c) Lord Maitreya
d) Mañjughoṣa
ANSWER: b

Q2. What reason does Śāntideva give for composing the text?
a) To benefit all sentient beings
b) To gain fame as a scholar
c) To develop his own understanding
d) To fulfill a request from his teacher
ANSWER: c

Q3. What does the commentary say is the purpose of Śāntideva's commitment to compose the text?

ANSWER: b

ANSWER: b

Q5. What analogy is used for the rare arising of merit and intelligence in the world?

Q6. According to the text, what is the only virtue that can overcome the great power of nonvirtue?

ANSWER: b

Q13. What happens from the moment one adopts the irreversible intent to liberate beings, even when asleep or distracted?

Q15. What does the text say about those who develop a malevolent intention towards the sons of the conquerors?
